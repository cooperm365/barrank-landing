The Spark

Born at the Bar

It started the way most good ideas do — just hanging out. Once Cooper was old enough to experience the bar scene, he became obsessed with the competitive energy of it — skill-based games, trash talk, the way a simple competition could pull a whole group together for hours.

But the experience always ended when you walked out the door. There was no way to carry that energy forward, no reason to come back to settle a score, no record of who actually dominated the night.

The Background

An Athlete's Mindset

Cooper's background has always revolved around athletics and a deep interest in entrepreneurship — two worlds that have more in common than most people realize. Both demand the same things: obsessive work ethic, the ability to compete under pressure, and the drive to keep going when things aren't working.

Building Bar Rank meant channeling exactly that — the same competitiveness and discipline required to perform in sports, applied to building something from scratch in an industry he genuinely loves.

"I wanted customers to feel like they were playing a game within a game every time they walked into a bar."

— Cooper Mattern, Founder

The Problem

Bars Deserve Better Tools

The bar and restaurant industry has a customer retention problem. Most venues have no real way to reward loyalty, track their regulars, or give customers a reason to come back beyond hoping the drinks are good. Traditional loyalty programs feel clunky and corporate — they don't fit the energy of a bar.

At the same time, modern marketing has left a lot of small bars behind. Big budgets, complicated platforms, and strategies built for retail don't translate. Bar owners need something that works the way their business works — fast, social, and built around the experience of actually being there.

The Solution

Loyalty That Feels Like Competition

Bar Rank turns every visit into a move in a larger game. Customers check in, earn points, and climb a live leaderboard — competing against other regulars for the top spot. Bars set challenges, offer rewards, and send SMS updates that keep people engaged between visits.

It's not a punch card. It's not a marketing blast. It's the feeling of having skin in the game every time you walk through the door — and a reason to come back and defend your rank.
